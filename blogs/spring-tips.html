Spring Heating Oil Tips

Published : 04/11/22

Avoid Moisture

If you live in New England, you're used to the cold spring nights and warmer days. While we love this weather it can cause problems with your oil tank as moisture may build up. The space between in your fuel tank between the fuel level and the top can cause condensation to build up. Condensation and moisture is the arch nemesis of metal because it causes it to corrode and rust. Overtime, if this gets worse you could have a leaky tank.

It is recommended when possible to fill your tank at the end of the winter season. This will significantly reduce the air gap between the current volume and the maximum capacity which thus, reduces the amount of condensation that can build up over time.

Off Peak Fill Up

Aside from avoiding moisture, an off-peak season fill up can be significantly cheaper as the demand is reduced. This could lead to potential savings which you could then use for further maintainence on your heating system to prevent major issues come the cold weather.

Schedule Tune Up

A routine tune up is like your annual physical with your doctor. A trained technician will come out, go over your system and perform a few checks to see how your system is operating. The technician may go through and repair or replace the following things, always check with your service provider to see what they cover.

  • Oil Filter Change - Replace or clean the oil filter to improve flow and efficency.
  • Flush Oil Line - Sediment happens. It can build up and clog up nozzles and lines which reduce burner efficency.
  • Oil Tank Inspection - Check for signs of damage or leaks which may lead to leaks or environmental or safety hazards.
  • Burner Nozzle Replacement - No one likes a dirty nozzle. It can cause a faulty flame leading to heat loss.
  • Furnace Air Filter Service - Cleaning or replacing the furnace air filter improves efficency which saves you money.
  • Blower Belt Check - Belts are checked, adjusted or replaced to ensure they're working properly.
  • Electrode Service - Electrodes carry an electrical current required for combustion of oil. They are adjusted or replaced as needed.
  • Burner Control Test - The burner is fired, controls are tested to make sure they're safely working.
  • Efficiency Test - Using a Bacharach Kit, the technician checks the percentage out of 100.
